About Me

First, let me introduce myself. My name is Vanessa Isabela Denny, and I am 29 years old. I was born and lived in Brazil all these years, and moved to Ottawa almost a month ago.

After finishing high school, I entered college to study Business Management and developed myself in the administrative area for just over six years; during these years I was always curious and excited to study and do extra work related to Information Systems. So, I returned to college to study Systems Analysis and Development where I was selected to be a math tutor to help other students, and I had the opportunity to do internships.

In my last job, I was able to gain experience with application development, where I stayed for two years and developed a new part of a bank mobile app based on projects by Customer Experience specialists. Working with them, I was able to understand the importance of analysis and research on interface design and user experience design, and in this exchange of knowledge I developed a great interest in learning more about design.

Then, I started the IMD program to continue sharpening my skills in the technology field and obtain great knowledge in design techniques and tools. Also, I am very interested to know the Canadian culture and improve my English skills, specially in communication.

One thing that I am passionate about is dancing. It is my 23th-year practicing this art, and I am looking forward to keep dancing here in Ottawa, ballet, jazz, and street dance. I was a professional dancer with scholarship in a company in Brazil for ten years and used to be a teacher for children, for no longer than five years and I had big challenges and opportunities like participating in a competition in Germany in 2015. It's something I don't really see myself getting away from for the rest of my life.


A film biography I really enjoyed watching was about Stephen Hawking, which has many wise teachings. One of the reflections that caught my attention the most was:

Remember to look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious. And however difficult life may seem, there is always something you can do and succeed at. It matters that you don't just give up. - Stephen Hawking
